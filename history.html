The History of Machine Learning

The concept of Machine Learning started in the 1950s with early artificial intelligence programs. In 1952, Arthur Samuel created one of the first learning programs, a checkers-playing AI.

During the 1980s and 1990s, advances in algorithms and computing power allowed ML to grow. Neural networks were developed, allowing computers to recognize patterns in data.

In the 2000s, the rise of big data and more powerful computers accelerated Machine Learning, leading to modern applications like self-driving cars, voice recognition, and recommendation systems.
